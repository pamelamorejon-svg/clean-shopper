# Clean Shopper

**Project:** Clean Shopper
**Date:** March 28, 2026
**Source:** Project brief — `CCDCourse_CleanShopper_ProjectBrief.md`
**Version:** 1.0

---

## 1. Problem Statement
People who want to make cleaner, safer purchasing decisions for their home and family cannot do so efficiently — product labels are technical and confusing, "clean" and "natural" marketing claims are unreliable, and researching alternatives across multiple sources is slow and fragmented. The problem is not lack of motivation; it is lack of a trustworthy, fast, and personalized way to act on it.

## 2. ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)
Someone who is already trying to reduce toxic products in their home — swapping out cleaning supplies, scrutinizing personal care ingredients, or shifting toward cleaner food options — but finds the research process too demanding to sustain. They are reading labels, searching review sites, and cross-referencing sources on their own today. They have accumulated opinions (brands they trust, ingredients they avoid) but no system to hold those preferences across shopping decisions. They are motivated but overwhelmed.

## 3. Pain Points
- Ingredient lists require specialized knowledge to interpret; most users don't have it
- "Clean," "natural," and "non-toxic" packaging claims are frequently misleading and unverified
- Researching alternatives means bouncing between multiple sources — search, review sites, databases — with no unified view
- Preferences and knowledge built up over time (trusted brands, flagged ingredients, meaningful certifications) have no persistent home and must be reconstructed each time

## 4. Proposed Solution
Users can:
- Describe what they're looking for in natural language and receive evaluated product recommendations with reasoning, drawn from real-time web search and ingredient safety databases (EWG Skin Deep)
- Save their preferences — ingredients to avoid, trusted brands, certifications that matter to them — so every future recommendation is filtered accordingly
- Add recommended products to a shopping cart that persists across sessions
- Ask for side-by-side product comparisons and receive a clear recommendation based on their saved preferences

# Collective Health Claims Pricing Engine (Zelis Replacement)

**Project:** Collective Health Claims Pricing Engine (Zelis Replacement)
**Date:** March 28, 2026
**Source:** Internal project discovery document (March 26 screenshot) + engineering proposal PDF — "Proposal: Provider Contracts and Claims Pricing" by lead engineer
**Version:** 2.0

---

## 1. Problem Statement
Collective Health prices and routes medical claims for PHP through a third-party vendor (Zelis Payer Compass / Visium) at a cost of $1.1M–$1.3M annually, supplemented by a second vendor (EHN) contracted specifically to assist with Zelis's complexity and training overhead. The system is a black box with no audit trail satisfying compliance requirements, no ability to customize pricing rules, and significant manual processing burden. What should be a core internal competency — pricing claims against contracts CH already owns — is entirely vendor-dependent, creating compounding cost, operational fragility, and a ceiling on the business's ability to evolve its contracting model.

## 2. ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)
Internal users across two teams:

**Claims/Pricing SMEs** who validate pricing output and manage edge cases: Joey Porath (project anchor, pricing/contract SME), John Forbes (Zelis cross-validation, locality/wage index, provider/contract matching), Maria Venderdahl (fee schedule QA and claim edit rules), Amelie Brisslinger (fee schedule QA), Cassie (claim-to-contract matching validation, Zelis experience).

**Engineering and operations** who build and maintain the pipeline: Sid (AI contract extraction, work already in progress), Harold Short and Chaith Pallapolu (claim data processing, downstream pipeline), Network Admin team (contract tracking and lifecycle management).

These users are currently navigating Zelis's portal manually, waiting weeks for contracts to load, working around data gaps, and operating without visibility into why any given claim was priced as it was.

## 3. Pain Points
- Zelis is a black box — no visibility into pricing logic at the claim line level; no audit trail for compliance
- Contract loading into Zelis Visium is manual, slow (weeks per contract), and requires specialized staff plus a second vendor (EHN) just to manage the process
- Limited ability to handle complex or non-standard contract types (multi-exhibit, value-based, capitation)
- Not all contracts have been converted to JSON/CSV format by Data Engineering — only those not yet loaded to Zelis were prioritized for AI agent work, leaving gaps in coverage
- Taxonomy code to Provider Specialty mapping is currently dependent on manual input from the provider ops team, creating a fragile data dependency
- No customization for CH-specific rules or edge cases within Zelis
- An active unresolved issue (CHILI-123) is a direct byproduct of these limitations
- Dependency on a vendor for a core financial function with no data ownership

## 4. Proposed Solution
The replacement is an incrementally deployed, internally owned pricing pipeline with three operational stages:

**Stage 1 — New internal API (transition architecture)**
An internal API is introduced that initially falls back to existing Zelis-based routing and pricing. It accepts Provider TIN and member network product (mapped via SBR04 segment) to match claims to contracts. When a contract match is found, the claim is treated as in-network; if Zelis pricing is missing, it loads to Ingenuity as an in-network claim. This runs in parallel with Zelis during the ramp.

**Stage 2 — AI Pricing Engine (5-phase deterministic execution)**
A Vertex AI Agent applies a fully deterministic 5-phase pricing path — no probabilistic guessing:
- **Phase 1:** Row-level rule extraction — matches procedure code to the contract's fee schedule row and extracts the specific CMS/RBRVS year (e.g., a single contract can price a medical line at 2019 rates and a behavioral health line at 2022 rates)
- **Phase 2:** Provider taxonomy and credentialing match — maps CH Provider Type to the contract's eligible practitioners; applies mid-level provider reductions (e.g., MA reimbursed at 75% of physician rate)
- **Phase 3:** CMS weightage and modifier resolution — calls CMS Physician Fee Schedule API for the year identified in Phase 1; applies Lab/Drug/Immunization fee schedules (rules received Feb 12, 2026) at higher precedence than the physician schedule; resolves modifier logic (Mod 26 professional, TC technical, global, bilateral Mod 50)
- **Phase 4:** Temporal rate selection — selects rate year 1 or year 2 based on claim AdmitDate
- **Phase 5:** Deterministic calculation via Python Interpreter — formula: `(CMS_Weight × Final_Factor × Line_Units) = Calculated_Allowed`, capped at Billed_Amount

**Exception handling:** Missing codes default to the "Standard/Miscellaneous" contract row; missing CMS weights flag for manual review; ambiguous years default to Contract Effective Date year; zero-unit lines auto-set to $0.00.

**Stage 3 — Zelis write-back (current PoC bridge)**
During the transition period, an RPA bot pushes the internally calculated allowed amounts back into Zelis Visium, updating claim status without manual keystrokes. This maintains Zelis as the system of record during the ramp while internal pricing accuracy is validated.

**Contract management**
Network Admin will gain tooling to track, manage, and update contracts over time. Claude Code is the designated tool for building and iterating on this operational tooling and workflow automation.

## 5. Success Metrics
- **Match rate:** >95% vs. Zelis output on repriced claims
- **Latency:** <50ms p99 per claim line
- **Cost savings:** $1.1M–$1.3M annually upon full Zelis decommission (plus elimination of EHN vendor costs)
- **Auditability:** Full PricingExplanation record per claim line
- **Rollout:** Gated traffic ramp — 1% → 5% → 10% → 25% → 50% → 100% live traffic

## 6. Design Constraints
**Platform:** Internal web tooling + agentic backend pipeline; conversational/agentic for pricing; operational dashboard for Network Admin
**Geography:** US healthcare market; CMS fee schedules imply US-only; no other regional constraints stated
**Accessibility:** Not defined in source material
**Technical:**
- AI pricing: Vertex AI Agent (deterministic execution, Python Interpreter Tool)
- Contract extraction: AI agent (Sid's existing work); contracts in Google Docs, JSON, and CSV formats
- Fee schedules: CMS Physician Fee Schedule API; Lab/Drug/Immunization schedule (Feb 12, 2026); RBRVS year-specific data
- Data pipeline: Python-based automation; `zelis_ready_for_review_with_ch_code.csv` as master audit file format
- Integration: SBR04 segment mapping; Provider TIN matching; RPA bot for Zelis write-back during transition
- Operational tooling: Claude Code for workflow automation and contract management UI
- Architecture diagram: Lucidchart (linked in proposal)
- Out of scope (V1): Checkout/payment, direct retailer integrations, barcode scanning, user accounts/authentication, mobile app
